A Bayesian model for hybrid Content-based Collaborative Filtering recommendation

2016-17 | Academic | Published | DOI: 10.1016/j.ijar.2018.02.001
Designed a novel, scalable, and interpretable Bayesian model which included textual description of the items for recommendation. The algorithm was evaluated on both public and real-life, commercial datasets in different recommendation settings, namely article recommendaiton and product recommendation. It showed noticeable improvement in prediction accuracy against other competitors with fast training time on CPU - thanks to optimization on convex-hull of the loss function. [Paper]

Published in an ISI-indexed journal, constantly ranked Q1/Q2 in Artificial Intelligence and Applied Mathematics

Multi-label Image classifcation with no Localization

2016 | Academic | Published | DOI: 10.1145/3011077.3011118
An early work that explored ConvNet for image tagging with single-scale input. The single-scale approach was different from e.g. R-CNN, YOLO framework in which multiple labels can be classified without extracting candidate regions before prediction, thus computational complexity is much lower at test time. Experiments on 2 small-scale datasets against previous work showed competitve classification accuracy, but fell short in rank-wise retrieval metrics - which was believed due to not training against cross-entropy instead of pair-wise loss function. [Paper] [Code]
